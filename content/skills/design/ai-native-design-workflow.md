Designers should **front-load problem framing** the way engineers front-load tests. Start from **user evidence** (quotes, clips, analytics) before prompting image or layout generators—otherwise you get slick **unmoored artifacts**.

**Research**: Use AI to **cluster interview themes** and **draft discussion guides**, then validate conclusions with humans. Maintain a **source-of-truth research repository** designers and PMs actually open.

**Ideation**: Run **diverge/converge** explicitly—generate many variants quickly, then **critique against heuristics and accessibility**. Time-box exploration; **pick directions** before high-fidelity polish.

**Mock & prototype**: Pair Figma (or equivalent) with **code or UI generators** for fast interactive stubs on novel patterns. Keep **design tokens** authoritative—AI outputs that ignore tokens become **tech debt overnight**.

**Handoff**: Produce **annotated specs**: states, motion intent, empty/error, localization notes. Record **short Looms** for complex flows. Treat dev handoff as **complete when acceptance tests exist**, not when PNGs are exported.

**Critique**: Hold weekly **design critique** with eng representation—focus on **user risk and feasibility**, not taste wars. Good AI-era design teams **ship learning milestones**, not only pixels.
