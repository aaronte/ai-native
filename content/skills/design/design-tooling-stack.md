Tool churn taxes **muscle memory, libraries, and procurement**. Pick a **core trio**: collaborative design files, **prototype/preview** surface, **handoff to code** path—then freeze for **one year** unless a tool materially fails SLAs.

**Evaluation**: Pilot on **real projects**, not toy files. Score **collaboration, component fidelity, dev-mode accuracy, a11y features, enterprise SSO, and export hygiene**. Include **legal review** for training policies if vendors use your files.

**Pairing model**: Figma remains **source of visual truth** for most teams; generators are **inputs** that must reconcile back to libraries. Avoid **two competing libraries**—pick one scaffold tool or fence its outputs.

**Enablement**: Maintain **internal cheat sheets** (“when to use X vs Y”). Someone must **own release notes** for plugins and AI features—broken plugins waste days at scale.

**Cost rationalization**: Count **active seats** vs. occasional viewers; migrate idle licenses quarterly. Negotiate **org-wide education** in contracts.

**Principle**: Tools should **increase decision quality**, not **decision quantity**. If a tool spams variants without learning loop, it’s misused.
