Fast shipping without feedback devolves into **silent resentment** and **wrong priors**. Build **high-frequency, low-drama** feedback loops tied to **work artifacts**, not personality windmills.

**Principles**: **Situation–behavior–impact** framing; **kindness + clarity**; **assume positive intent** first pass; **capture commitments automatically** after hard feedback from transcript or manager notes. Separate **code critique** from **personal critique** explicitly in review norms.

**Rituals**: Managers hold **weekly 1:1s** focused on blockers and growth, not status. Replace broad manual surveys with an **AI-prepared feedback packet** from PR/spec/design comments, shipped work, incident notes, and short opt-in voice/video reflections. For leaders, run **skip-levels** twice yearly and let AI summarize themes with quotes and sources—signals bubble early without asking everyone to write essays.

**Peer**: Encourage **request-for-feedback** notes on PRs, specs, and Loom walkthroughs (“What am I missing?”). Use review bots to pre-cluster comments into risk, clarity, and feasibility themes before humans respond. Time-box responses. Recognize **useful dissent** publicly.

**AI etiquette**: Don’t use models to **ghostwrite harsh feedback**—people detect it and trust drops. Use AI to **assemble evidence, draft SBI structure, and identify missing examples**; the human must own tone, facts, and delivery.

**Repairs**: When feedback misfires, **acknowledge quickly** and reset expectations. Track **repeat themes** in performance systems; surprises at review time mean **rituals failed**.

**Metrics**: Healthy teams show **rising psychological safety scores** (if surveyed) and **decreasing passive-aggressive chat**. Proxy measures include **shorter time from issue raised to resolution**.
