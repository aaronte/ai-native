Speed without cadence becomes **noise**. Cadence is how execs **steer without micromanaging** and teams **protect focus time**.

**Weekly**: Squad **planning + demo + retro lite** (30–45 minutes each, time-boxed) should start from an **AI-generated brief**: changed tickets, merged PRs, customer signals, open risks, and decisions needed. Leadership **metrics standup** covers only anomalies from dashboards, not full decks. **Customer signal review** should use AI-clustered support themes, call clips, NPS verbatims, and product telemetry.

**Monthly**: **Cross-team dependency forum** should be seeded by an agent that finds blocked tickets, stale dependencies, and repeated handoff comments. **Portfolio review**: live dashboard of bets on track, kill list, and confidence changes. **Tooling health check**: cost, incidents, adoption, and satisfaction pulled from systems of record.

**Quarterly**: **Strategy refresh** tied to evidence: market, metrics, capacity, and AI-summarized customer/revenue shifts. **Talent & skills gap** review should combine shipped-work evidence, manager notes, and learning activity before asking leaders for subjective ratings. **Security & compliance** checkpoint should use automated access reviews, data maps, and policy exceptions.

**What changes with AI**: More **async captured context**, shorter **live meetings**, stricter **auto-updated decision logs**. Increase **slack capacity** visibly each quarter until throughput stabilizes.

**Executive participation**: Leaders attend **demos**, read **decision logs**, and **kill projects** decisively. Nothing signals priority like **calendar allocation**.

**Anti-patterns**: **Boilerplate OKRs** that don’t connect to weekly work. **Status tourism** across too many initiatives. Replace with **fewer, clearer bets**.

**Remote/hybrid**: Rotate times, record decisions, and publish **Loom/video recaps with AI summaries** after leadership meetings; the source recording, summary, owners, and decision log should link to the same canonical page.
