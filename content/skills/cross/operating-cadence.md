Speed without cadence becomes **noise**. Cadence is how execs **steer without micromanaging** and teams **protect focus time**.

**Weekly**: Squad **planning + demo + retro lite** (30–45 minutes each, time-boxed). Leadership **metrics standup**—only anomalies, not full decks. **Customer signal review** (support themes, NPS verbatims).

**Monthly**: **Cross-team dependency forum**—surface blockers early. **Portfolio review**: bets on track? kill list? **Tooling health check** (cost, incidents, satisfaction).

**Quarterly**: **Strategy refresh** tied to evidence: market, metrics, capacity. **Talent & skills gap** review—hire or train? **Security & compliance** checkpoint (access reviews, data maps).

**What changes with AI**: More **async prep**, shorter **live meetings**, stricter **decision logs**. Increase **slack capacity** visibly each quarter until throughput stabilizes.

**Executive participation**: Leaders attend **demos**, read **decision logs**, and **kill projects** decisively. Nothing signals priority like **calendar allocation**.

**Anti-patterns**: **Boilerplate OKRs** that don’t connect to weekly work. **Status tourism** across too many initiatives. Replace with **fewer, clearer bets**.

**Remote/hybrid**: Rotate times, record decisions, and use **single written summaries** after every leadership meeting.
