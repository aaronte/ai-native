**Days 1–30 — Foundations.** Establish **guardrails**: approved tools, data policies, secret handling, and review standards. Pull **baseline metrics** automatically (lead time, rework, incidents, reviewer load, design handoff lag). Name **tool owners** in eng, design, and PM. Deliver **starter prompts, bot commands, and generated templates** plus a single internal source of truth that agents can update from accepted artifacts.

**Days 31–60 — Pilots.** Select 1–2 teams for **bounded experiments** with weekly AI-generated pilot briefs, not manual check-ins. Success = **faster delivery without quality regression** and lightweight pulse feedback from pilot members. Kill scope creep—**one primary use case** per pilot (e.g., test generation, spec drafting from Looms, component scaffolding).

**Days 61–90 — Scale & systematize.** Publish **playbooks** from pilots: what worked, anti-patterns, example diffs. Roll training to **all engineers and designers**; managers get **office hours**. Integrate AI usage into **onboarding**. Tie incentives to **outcomes**, not raw usage quotas.

**Exit criteria each phase**: Publish **hard stops** in the rollout dashboard—if incidents spike or reviews bottleneck, **pause expansion** and fix process. Communicate wins and failures with short Looms, AI summaries, and links to the source metrics.

**Leadership**: Executives **model behavior** or delegate visibly. Fund **enablement** (partial headcount) so teams aren’t learning entirely on nights and weekends.
