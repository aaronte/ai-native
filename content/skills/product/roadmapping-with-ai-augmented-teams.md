AI-native teams often see **spikey throughput**: sudden bursts and unexpected rework. Roadmaps must become **portfolio bets with explicit slack**, not fixed Gantt fantasies.

**Bet sizing**: Classify initiatives as **S/M/L** with **time horizons** (this quarter / next / horizon). Cap **concurrent L bets** per squad—large work absorbs review and incident capacity silently.

**Slack**: Reserve **15–25% capacity** for tech debt, reliability, and surprise work. If you plan 100% feature utilization, **any bug bash or model regression** blows dates. Show slack **explicitly** to leadership; hiding it creates cynicism.

**Re-baselining**: Move from single-point estimates to **ranges** (earliest / likely / latest). Revisit **mid-quarter** when telemetry shows velocity shifts. Publish **what we stopped** when you add work—roadmaps communicate **trade-offs**, not wishes.

**Outcomes over output**: Tie epics to **measurable behavior change**. If engineering ships fast but adoption flatlines, **slow feature adds** and invest in onboarding or pricing. Velocity without impact is a **strategy failure**, not an eng failure.

**Stakeholder rhythm**: Monthly **exec snapshot**: three shipped outcomes, two learnings, one risk. Avoid death-by-roadmap-review slides; **link to live dashboards**.
