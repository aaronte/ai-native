When eng and design also use AI, **decision latency** becomes the constraint. PMs must **compress handoff cycles** and **make constraints visible early**.

**Definition of Ready**: Before kicking off, align on **problem, target user, success metric, guardrails (perf, compliance), and launch tier**. Missing any item invites **rounds of AI-generated rework**.

**Rituals**: Keep **weekly triad time** (PM/Eng/Design) sacred—short, decision-oriented. Replace status theater with **shared board hygiene**: blocked columns, WIP limits, explicit owners. Use **Loom** for complex walkthroughs; **transcript → ticket bullets**.

**Async norms**: Default response SLAs by severity—**P0 same hour**, **P1 same day**. Use **structured threads** (“Decision needed: A vs B by Thu”). Avoid @channel pings without **summary + link**.

**AI etiquette**: When PMs use assistants for **draft specs or emails**, label **assumptions** and **open questions** clearly so partners don’t debate fiction. Same for eng generated summaries—**link sources**.

**Anti-bottleneck**: If PM approval gates every microcopy change, **delegate** within guardrails (tone guidelines, legal pre-approved blocks). Scale PM leverage with **principles and examples**, not central bottlenecks.

**Trust**: Celebrate **disagree-and-commit** moments. Cross-functional speed comes from **shared reality on evidence**, not consensus on everything.
